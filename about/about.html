WHY EMBRACE

LGBTQ is an acronym for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender and queer or questioning. These terms are used to describe a person’s sexual orientation or gender identity. According to the survey conducted by HRC Foundation and the University of Connecticut in 2018, there are more than 12,000 LGBTQ teenagers across the United States.   

LGBTQ  teenager may experience high levels of anxiety at home, at school, in social circles and communities. Often, they cannot get enough help and support from people around them.

Embrace connects these teenagers to qualified mentors from our partner LGBTQ organizations. After each teenager gets paired up with a mentor, they will be able to express their feelings and get advice from someone who is experienced and empathetic.

So, if you ever need help, don't hesitate to reach out. We are here to Embrace you!

Our Partners

All mentors at Embrace are carefully selected. They come from LGBTQ organizations across the country such as The Human Rights Campaign and The LGBT National Help Center. They are willing to listen and offer advice.
